Research

Doctoral Research - IAFE (CONICET)

I started my PhD in Physics in 2026, focusing on the interaction between the solar wind and planetary environments, with particular emphasis on Mars. My research combines space plasma physics, magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), and machine learning techniques to analyze magnetic field data from space missions such as MAVEN.

The project aims to develop data-driven methods to identify and characterize key plasma boundaries — such as the Martian bow shock and magnetic pile-up boundary — and to understand their response to solar wind variability. This work integrates physical modeling with advanced computational tools, including time-series analysis and classification algorithms.

Beyond its scientific contribution to planetary space physics, the research strengthens expertise in data analysis, signal processing, numerical modeling, and machine learning applied to complex real-world systems.
